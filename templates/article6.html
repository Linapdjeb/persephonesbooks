1. The Perks Of Being A Wallflower, by Stephen Chbosky


Shop Now!

The Perks of Being a Wallflower is a young adult coming-of-age epistolary novel by American writer Stephen Chbosky, which was first published on February 1, 1999, by Pocket Books.

Fifteen-year-old Charlie is coping with the suicide of his friend, Michael. To lessen the fear and anxiety of starting high school alone, Charlie starts writing letters to a stranger, someone he heard was nice but has never met in person.



2. The Book Thief, by Markus Zusak


Shop Now!

The Book Thief is a historical novel by Australian author Markus Zusak, and is his most popular work. It tells the story of Liesel, a little girl who is taken to a new home because her mother can't afford to take care of her. The story is told by Death, who becomes a character you come to respect and even feel sorry for by the end.



3. The Fault in Our Stars, by John Green


Shop Now!

The Fault In Our Stars is a fabulous book about a young teenage girl who has been diagnosed with lung cancer and attends a cancer support group. Hazel and Augustus embark on a roller coaster ride of emotions, including love, sadness and romance, while searching for the author of their favourite book.



4. The Catcher In The Rye, by J. D. Salinger


Shop Now!

The novel details two days in the life of 16-year-old Holden Caulfield after he has been expelled from prep school. Confused and disillusioned, Holden searches for truth and rails against the “phoniness” of the adult world. He ends up exhausted and emotionally unstable.
One of my all time favorite.



5. The Hate U Give, by Angie Thomas


Shop Now!

The Hate U Give is Angie Thomas's first novel about a teenage girl who grapples with racism, police brutality, and activism after witnessing her black friend murdered by the police. The book became an immediate young adult bestseller and was adapted into a movie shortly after its release.
It is Thomas's debut novel, expanded from a short story she wrote in college in reaction to the police shooting of Oscar Grant.



6. One Of Us Is Lying, by Karen M. McManus


Shop Now!

One of Us is Lying is a young adult, mystery/suspense novel by American author Karen M. McManus. It follows the gripping story of Bronwyn, Addy, Nate, and Cooper as suspects in the murder of Simon Kelleher. Each of the high school students have secrets that they would do anything to protect, so how far would they go to make sure they're kept out of the spotlight?



7. The Outsiders, by S. E. Hinton


Shop Now!

The Outsiders is a coming-of-age novel by S. E. Hinton, first published in 1967 by Viking Press. The novel tells the story of Ponyboy Curtis and his struggles with right and wrong in a society in which he believes that he is an outsider. Ponyboy and his two brothers — Darrel (Darry), who is 20, and Sodapop, who is 16 — have recently lost their parents in an automobile accident.



8. Aristotle And Dante Discover The secrets Of The Universe, by Benjamin Alire Sáenz


Shop Now!

Aristotle and Dante Discover the Secrets of the Universe is a coming-of-age young adult lyrical novel about family and friendship from critically acclaimed author Benjamin Alire Sáenz.
Aristotle is an angry teen with a brother in prison. Dante is a know-it-all who has an unusual way of looking at the world. When the two meet at the swimming pool, they seem to have nothing in common.



9. All The Bright Places, by


Shop Now!

After meeting each other, two people struggle with the emotional and physical scars of their past. They discover that even the smallest moments can mean something.
All the Bright Places is a compelling and beautiful story about a girl who learns to live from a boy who intends to die. Theodore Finch is fascinated by death, and he constantly thinks of ways he might kill himself. But each time, something good, no matter how small, stops him.



10. The Conference of the Birds (Miss Peregrine's Peculiar Children), by Ransom Riggs


Shop Now!

With enemies behind him and the unknown ahead, Jacob Portman’s story continues as he takes a brave leap forward into The Conference of the Birds, the fifth novel in the bestselling Miss Peregrine’s Peculiar Children series by Ransom Riggs.
The Conference of the Birds continues the series Miss Peregrine's Peculiar Children, as Jacob Portman tries to follow through on a mission given to him by his dying grandfather. He must help fulfill a prophecy, or certain doom will befall both the normal and peculiar worlds.
